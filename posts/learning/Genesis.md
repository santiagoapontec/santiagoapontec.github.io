> In the beginning, God created the Heavens and the earth. the earth was without form and void, and the darkness was over the face of the deep. And the spirit of God was hovering over the face of the waters.
> And God said, "Let there be light", and there was light. And God saw that the light was good. And God separated the light from the darkness. God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And there was evening and there was morning, the firsy day

Darkness was the initial state of the world (the earth), it was God who had to say _"Let there be light"_. This is also the first time where we see that God saw something was _good_ - light was the first thing (that we know of) that God deemed to be good.

The coming of light is also the first time we have contrast in the Bible. God separates light and darkness and calls one Day and the other he calls Night - it's important to note that there's no apparent explicit condemnation of darkness here, we do we however know that light is good, accoridng to God.

> And God said "Let there be an expanse in the midst of the waters, and let it separate the waters from the waters." And God made the expanse and separated the waters that were under the expanse from the waters that were above the expanse. And it was so. And God called the expanse Heaven. And there was evening and there was morning, the second day.

This passage confuses me a little bit now since in the earlier passage we read that God created the heavens and the earth, and now we read that after he created the expanse he called it heaven.

A quick Google search explains this in two ways:

1. The first we read, which in a Bible would be Gensis 1:1 is only a summary of the entirity of Genesis - that is, _"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth"_. The rest of the chapter is just the detailed explanation as to how this happened.

2. Theb original word used for "heaven" in the Bible was the hebrew word _"Shamayim"_, which is plural, and therefore may be describing different layers of God's creation. Apparently through the Bible we see three heavens described. 1) What we would call the sky, which is where birds fly and rain falls. 2) What we would describe as outer space, which is the resting place of of the sun, the stars and the moon. 3) The highest heaven, which some understand as the dwelling place of God.
