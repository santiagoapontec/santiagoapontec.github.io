An actual record so that I can't lie on podcasts down the road...

May 31st

I sometimes say that I'm going through my mid 40's crisis in my 20's.

I say that to justify the fact that maybe all this thinking and all this misery is going to serve me in the long-run because I will have figured most off the things that people never think about and then suddenly get hit by - it's me thinking that I'm front-loading all the suffering instead of enjoying the bliss that comes with ignorance.

But sometimes I think it goes too far, sometimes the load from all that thinking is so unbearably heavy that it yanks me to my knees and puts me at a full stop. The funny thing is that I sort of bring it upon myself - sure, there's some truth behind it, but it's also me who chooses to give mental space to these things when I'd be better off just working.

I find that writing helps me tame down the feeling - helps me come back to my feet, so to say. So, even though I've tamed the feeling by watching YouTube shorts already, I thought I'd give this a try.

It usually starts with some thought regarding the future. It's mostly about money.

Today that started as me thinking that I realistically only need about two-thousand or so dollars to live the kind of life where money isn't an opposing force to freedom. It's the sum that breaks the subordination my time offers in exchange for money. I'm working on a project now, which has some decent chance of maybe making me a couple of bucks, but maybe not in the most sustainable of ways - perhaps I shouldn't be putting this online, and maybe one day I'll wipe this clean and replace this line if it all does work out, but trying to beat the casino's at their own game isn't the most stress-free life I can imagine.

Anyways, I think about the fact that the project will work out and so I'll start making some good monthly income from it and be able to give myself that freedom - but then I start thinking about the fact that the money flow has to theoretically be stable over a very long period of time for me to have a chance for that freedom to materialize itself as something useful - idle time to work on things I think are interesting. And of course the thought comes that beating the casino's at their own game only probably works for so long before you want to stop doing it or have to stop doing it...

So then I think about how to maximize the profit from casino's to buy me at least a free year. But then when I get the free year the clock is ticking and I need to find some other way of making money.

And so maybe the optimal path is just optimizing to get the things one needs to live a stress-free life. What do we all chase our whole lives? It's a car and house. We work for our mortgage and car payments. So maybe that's what I need to optimize for - squeeze enough money to buy me a plot of land in the countryside with a small house and an old truck, then I'll be set.

But, gosh, house-prices are crazy - four-hundred thousand dollars for a house with a good plot of land in the country? That's a hell of a lot of money. But then, it really isn't, four-hundred-thousand dollars, can I not figure out a way to make that? Surely it's a skill issue.

Four-hundred-thousand dollars. I could've made than in two years if I had managed to stay in the states and get that job I was too scared to do. So am I wasting my time by working on the project? Per unit of time it may take me the same time as a job...but, well, is the whole point just getting the house, or is it also what you do in order to get the house? Because that sounds precisely like why I told myself I quit, I don't want to give up two years of my life for a paycheck...

And well, all that happens, and you know what? Time is ticking on my free year right now...that project which this whole made-up anxiety runs on? It's yet to be done...

God is it easy to get carried away.

I know that isn't it. That's not the way to do it.

But there's a way where both get taken care of - there's both a sensible plan that alleviates that and a lot of action.

Action is probably the key of that mix though.

If I indeed want to have all those problems I should be building a life which allows me to face them, and that happens in action, not in thought.

It's genuinely fucking incredible how many times I've gotten to that conclusion. The volume alone should tell me something.

But what's wrong if I keep getting to the same conclusion? Is it as easy as you're not acting? Is it precisely that why the conclusion just always ends up being to act?

Probably so, but also, probably not.

But, at this point we're served better by usefulness than by truth. So yes, yes it is precisely that, I just need to act.
