11:09 PM - I just watched the New York Knicks win their first championship in 53 years - it was incredible.

It's one of the only times in my adult life where I can say I've been genuinely happy to see someone win.

No one can take this away from these guys ever - they'll wake up tomorrow having made history. Legacy cemented.

This is the first time I've seen it live, but there are many instances of that moment.

It's your packed stadium concert as a musician. People singing your song without you.

It's lifting the world cup.

It's your speech at the oscars.

I just hope and pray that one day I'll get to feel that feeling.

And you know, it's about work too.

Can we all experience that feeling? I don't know. Could we all work our way to it? I hope so.

It's hard to say this is a defining moment for me - I've said it too many times before for it to be special now.

But I don't want to spend my days looking at YouTube shorts anymore, I don't want to spend my days avoiding work because I'm scared of facing it. I don't want to spend my days dreaming of the feeling.

I want to spend my days building towards it.
