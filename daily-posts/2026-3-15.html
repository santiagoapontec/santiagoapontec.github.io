March 15th

I’m 23 years old, living at home with my parents, and writing essays for a page I’ve told not many people about1 — that hit me today.

Some not-so-old version of my2 mind would’ve gone running into the comfort of an exciting new plan — Getting a new job. Moving to some remote town and trying to be a waiter. Signing-up for ironman. Getting home and starting to plan my new start-up. Blah, blah, blah.

But today, I just sat with that discomfort. Not in the prettiest of ways, not completely unfaced by it — just sat with it, while staring at the restaurant table.

I’m slowly starting to understand what I’ve known for a long time. There’s no easy way out — not for what I want to do. I want to fully face myself, I want to know me and change me — and I don’t get to do that by running. I get to do that by facing the grim reality that I'm just a 23 year old guy, living at home with his parents, writing essays for a page he’s told no one about.

And that’s okay, because life is all about trade-offs, and I’m happy with mine.3 I know that while there may be short-term comfort in living a different life — one where I lean in into being the 23 year old bachelor with money and his own flat — it’s this one that will satisfy me long-term — not just if it works out, but if it doesn’t too. In the end, all I care about is feeling like I took the bet I wanted to take, not the one I felt I needed to.

It’s only up to me to finish this leaving like I had nothing left to give, throwing all my chips in the middle, so to say.

Only then will I be able to rest easily at night4 — only then will I be able to move on with no regrets.

– –

To that point, that’s what we do tomorrow — we get up and do the boring work required to make reality a little less grim.

For now, that looks like consistently doing a couple of things;

  1. Running
  2. Weight Lifting
  3. Studying Math
  4. Updating this page — with both essays and study journals
  5. Remembering that while doing this feels necessary, none of it will fix me – it’s only God who can do that.5

I’ll make it a point to write about these on each day, keep you updated on my consistency and progress in these — along with my thoughts as usual, of course.

- Santi

(1) And it's not my plan, really. If you’re reading this, either you’re a complete internet stranger to me (in which case; hey, hope the page has been sorta good for you), or you’re a close friend of mine — I think there’s no point in “promoting” the page, since my goal is not to become famous or make money from this, it’s simply to have a living journal of my progress in the hopes that it’s useful for me and perhaps someone else in the future.
